UX Thinking: Variant 1

This variation focuses on Visual Chunking. By placing the filters inside a large container with distinct "blocks", it creates a clear sense of hierarchy. The company boxes are the primary touchpoint, using large hit areas for better ergonomics. The Experience Level uses pill-shaped buttons that act as "modes", while the country list uses a horizontal scroll to suggest an expansive list of locations without cluttering the vertical space.

UX Thinking: Variant 2

This variation utilizes an L-Shaped Layout to maximize information density while keeping the experience "soft". By moving companies to a side panel, we free up vertical space for titles and locations. The titles use a "Badge-with-Separator" style which is very common in modern SaaS products, making the job count feel like a metadata point rather than a separate label. The use of varied pastel backgrounds (Emerald, Amber, Purple) for countries helps the user visually distinguish between different regions instantly.

UX Thinking: Variant 3 (Compact)

This variation is designed for **Vertical Real-Estate Efficiency**. By using horizontal "ribbons" and a smaller font size (`text-[11px]`), we can fit all 3 levels of filters in less than 300px of height. It uses vertical label markers to separate the rows, creating a "Dashboard" look that is perfect for tool-heavy applications.

UX Thinking: Variant 4 (Ultra-Compact)

This variation uses a **Grid Column Approach** to keep the filter group extremely compact. Instead of wide rows, it breaks the filters into three vertical buckets. This is ideal for users who know exactly what they want and need to scan all filters at a single glance without moving their eyes across the entire screen width. The use of highly saturated accent colors for labels (Rose, Sky, Amber) provides instant semantic separation.

UX Thinking: Variant 5 (Neon Night)

This variation moves away from light mode to explore a **Dark-Playful Aesthetic**. Dark modes are often perceived as more "premium" and "tech-focused" for engineering jobs. It uses **Glassmorphism** (semi-transparent backgrounds) and neon accents to highlight selections. The compact nature comes from removing labels and relying on iconography and consistent padding to imply the categories.
